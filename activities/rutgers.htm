Climate Survey
From 2015 to 2019, I organized a biennial climate survey for the Rutgers philosophy department. The climate survey gauged the atmosphere of the Rutgers philosophy department among graduate students in general, and among graduate students who are members of underrepresented groups in particular. Please feel free to contact me if you would like more information, or if you would like advice on how to set up and run a climate survey in your own department.

Rutgers-Columbia Workshop: Quantum Field Theories
This workshop featured talks on the metaphysical status of quantum field theory by David Baker, Ward Struyve, Tian Yu Cao, Anna Ijjas, Porter Williams, Laura Ruetsche, Michael Miller, and David Wallace. It was organized by Eddy Chen, Sebastien Rivat, and Isaac Wilhelm; and it was funded by Marc Sanders Foundation, Rutgers University Philosophy Department, Columbia University Philosophy Department, Rutgers Graduate Student Association. For more information, visit the workshop website.

Metro Area Philosophy of Science
Every semester, MAPS hosts three to five lectures in the New York, New Jersey area on topics in the philosophy of science. It has been organized by Diego Arana, Denise Dykstra, Isaac Wilhelm, Eddy Chen, and Porter Williams. It is sponsored by the philosophy departments at Columbia, CUNY, NYU, and Rutgers. For information about upcoming talks, visit the MAPS website or facebook page.

Seminar on History and Foundations of Probability and Statistics
Many disciplines use probability theory, including mathematical statistics, philosophy, and physics. The goal of this seminar, which began at Rutgers University in 2016, was to help people in these disciplines learn from each other and from the history of probability and its applications. The organizers included Xueyin Zhang, Glenn Shafer, Barry Loewer, Virgil Murthy, Tripp Roberts, Harry Crane, Ruobin Gong, Eddy Keming Chen, Isaac Wilhelm, and Dimitris Tsementzis.
