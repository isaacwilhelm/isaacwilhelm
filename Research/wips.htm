Works in Progress or Under Review

Higher-Order Metaphysics

Identity by Grounding (grounding, identity of propositions)

I derive a complete theory of propositional identity from a few plausible principles of grounding.

Talk About Types (logic, type theory, identity, absolute generality)

I argue that for the purposes of metaphysical theorizing, the calculus of constructions—a higher-order language which allows for quantification over types—is better than other languages discussed by philosophers.

The Stage Theory of Properties and Propositions (properties, propositions, logic)

I propose a stage-theoretic, fine-grained theory of properties, relations, and propositions, which avoids the Russell-Myhill argument.

Typed Composition as Typed Identity (composition, identity, type theory)

I use the simply typed lambda calculus to formulate a non-trivial, consistent theory of composition as identity.

Social Metaphysics

Gender is Essential to Some, But Not All, Individuals (feminist philosophy, essence)

I argue that some individuals have their genders essentially while others do not.

Pluralities, Counterparts, and Groups (groups, social entities)

I formulate a theory of groups based on pluralities and counterparts, and I compare it to another theory of groups which I have developed (the `stage theory'; forthcoming in AJP).

Philosophy of Science/Physics

Centering the Everett Interpretation (philosophy of physics, interpretations of quantum mechanics)

I use centered changes—chances, that is, of centered propositions—to formulate an account of probability in the Everett interpretation of quantum mechanics.

Explanatory Circles (explanation, physics, special sciences)

I give three arguments for the view that there are perfectly good, scientifically legitimate, circular explanations.

Grounds for Effective Theories (grounding, quantum field theories, renormalization group)

I propose a ground-theoretic account of the physical content, and the non-fundamental character, of effective quantum field theories.

Reduction, Autonomy, and the Special Sciences (special science properties, grounding, perfect naturalness)

I propose an account of special science properties which (i) respects their autonomy from fundamental physical properties, and yet (ii) implies that they reduce to fundamental physics.

The Democratic View, Extreme Crises, and Values in Science (non-epistemic values, trust, science)

I argue that in some cases of extreme crisis, the non-epistemic values guiding scientific research should not be chosen democratically.

The Physical Content of Mathematical Expressions (philosophy of physics, theory interpretation)

I explore three principles for extracting physical content from the mathematical expressions in which physical theories are couched.

Other

Causation is Explanation (causation, explanation)

I show how causation can be reductively analyzed in terms of explanation.

Grounding to Supervenience (perception, type theory)

I show that a plausible supervenience principle can be derived from standard principles of grounding.

Perception, Content, and Character (perception, type theory)

I use the simply typed lambda calculus to propose an analysis of the content, and phenomenal character, of perceptual states.

The Objective Value of Naturalness (perfectly natural properties, value)

I propose a theory of the objective value of natural properties and relations, and I defend that theory against prominent criticisms due to Dasgupta and Hirsch.
